Project Description

OVERVIEW

Topic

An overview of what you propose to do in your project. Concentrate on the big picture and outcomes, rather than intricate details. At least two paragraphs is expected.

This project aims to create and monetise a web application that acts as an intermediary between both businesses that deal with advertisements, branding, and marketing, and between those that develop and design video games.

Upon launch the web application will provide a service that will allow for the insertion of real-world advertising material into video games, in real-time. Advertising materials will be uploaded onto the service, and strategically served into video games for viewing by consumers based on predetermined metrics. The service with be monetised through a commission-based model where video game developers will be paid based on the how frequently advertising material is viewed in-game.


MOTIVATION

What are your motivations for your project. Why is this project important or interesting? How does it fit in with current IT trends? What would it show to a future employer if you were able to work on this project? At least one paragraph is expected.

For this project, our group aimed to conceptualise an idea that aligned with overlapping interests from all group members. We agreed this project would be interesting and valuable as it is an overlap between the fun pastime of video games, the large world of advertising and marketing, and the technical intricacies of programming and information technology.

These fields of Entertainment, Marketing, and Information Technology are all areas our group is interested in, and they are also very well-established with plenty of resources available. This gives us room to build and develop an exciting new service that combines elements from each field and allows us to develop further understanding in these areas.

This project will aim to allow video game developers to send and receive advertising information and analytics in an efficient way, using a custom API. API’s are becoming ever more prevalent in the current IT landscape as they allow for consistent and predefined methods of sharing data. As video gaming has been slowly shifting towards an ‘always online’ experience, we feel it is time to utilise this connectivity to integrate video gaming into the world of advertising.

By developing this project, we hope to showcase our ability to utilise Information Technology in innovative ways to further monetise the video game industry.


LANDSCAPE

While overarching idea of our project already exists in the form of In-Game Advertising (IGA) and more specifically Dynamic In-Game Advertising (DIGA), however we believe the methodology and implementation leaves much to be desired.

The only real ‘competitor’ that claims to offer Dynamic In-Game Advertising is a an organisation named RapidFire. RapidFire is a digital marketing company that specialises in in-game advertising, and operates via a consultation based method.

RapidFire offer Dynamic In-Game Advertising and they tout this advertising channel as a real-time service that can provide advertising in video games in “the form of placements such as billboards, posters, and hoardings situated throughout a 3D video game environment.”[1].

Our project and delivery would differ slightly, as we would aim to be an easily accessible online platform where brands can quickly set up their own campaigns, upload materials, and have them be streamed directly into games. We would aim to remove as much of the consultation requirements as possible by providing a seamless platform that does most of the heavy lifting.

[1] https://www.rapidfire.com/portfolio/dynamic-in-game-advertising/

Plans and Progress

The Story of How it all began…

In our first MS Teams meeting dating back to our first team assignment, we went around as a group and exchanged what our ideas were for our assignment 1 project idea. Marcus shared with us his genius, imaginative blueprint of a web service connecting game developers with brands to create a fresh new approach to advertising in games. In our second team assignment we discussed different technologies and techniques that’d help manifest the idea.

Imagining the website from the clients perspective is an incredibly efficient way to determine how the website should visually present itself to ensure a flawless intuitive product. Working backwards by designing the website and determining the mechanics afterwards.

To use the website, a representative from either a game developer or brand needs to register an account. There are two types of accounts, one for the game developer and another for the brand.

Game Developer Account
If a game developer wishes to register with the website they will follow the respective prompt after following the “Register” link. There they will pick the “Game Developer” option and will be lead to a a page where they are required to fill out necessary information.

  • First Name
  • Last Name
  • Email
  • Password
  • Confirm Password
  • Two factor Authentication Code
  • What game developer branch they are representing
  • Arrange Payment Details

Once they have filled out the mandatory information, they’ll now have an account on the website where they now have the ability to list a new advertisement vacancy available to rent.

There’ll be a “+ New Listing “link in the header to direct the game developer to a form that they’ll fill out to create a new listing. In the “New Ad Listing” tab, the game developer will be asked to supply the following.

Game Title
What game the advertising space will be featured on.

Advertisement Type
Advertisement types can be both ‘2D’ and ‘3D’, in a sense that flat images like ground stickers and billboards will be jpeg, gif and png files while 3D files will be CAD files. Information regarding the necessary dimensions and size of the file will be outlined within the same section of ‘Advertisement Type’

Monthly Players
Game developers need to provide realistic, truthful figures on how many unique monthly players they receive so the potential brand that wishes to advertise on their listing can gauge if the price is reasonable or not.

User Statistics
Similar to the monthly players, user statistics should be required but aren’t mandatory for the initial stages. Users statistics will include geographical location of players, their age, gender etc. This is included so advertisers can accurately direct their product at specific groups of people.

Asking Price
Required to determine the price of renting the available promotional space. Game developers also need to specify if the price is on a daily, weekly, monthly basis.

Description
A description is vital for the game developer to communicate what it is they are offering. Here they’ll explain the rental space and may even mention what the game is about.

Images
Lastly, the game developer needs to upload pictures to the potential brand customer knows where and what their advertisement will look like. After all the information is filled in they will click a button to list the advertising space.

Brand Account
Brand accounts are responsible for purchasing the rental spaces from the game developers.

If a brand/ company wishes to register with the website they will follow the respective prompt after following the “Register” link. There they will pick the “Brand/ Company” option and will be lead to a a page where they are required to fill out necessary information.

  • First Name
  • Last Name
  • Email
  • Password
  • Confirm Password
  • Two factor Authentication Code
  • What brand / company they are representing
  • Arrange Payment Details

Now that the person representing a brand has created a brand account, they now have the ability to purchase advertising space from game developers. Back on the homepage a brand representative will click on an advertisement listing proposed by a game developer.

After clicking the listing they’ll be able to view a neat outline of the details regarding the advertising space.

  • Game title
  • Advertisement Type + Required Specs
  • Monthly Players
  • User Statistics
  • Asking price
  • Description
  • Image

Additionally, the brand representative will have the option to message the game developer representative to clarify any questions they may have. Once they’re happy they’ll add the space to their shopping cart where they can now go to the checkout to fill in the last details.

The checkout will require the brand representative to upload the appropriate file type with the dimensions considered. After uploading said file, they will need to determine the duration they wish to advertise their brand for. A final cost will be calculated based upon the duration multiplied by the rate supplied by the game developer. Once that’s done, they’ll proceed to pay with a preferred payment method (either paypal, bank transfer, cryptocurrency).

Once the payment has been made, the file will be ported onto the game's API endpoint, where it will now be displayed and advertised within the game.

Scopes and Limits

For a project like this there is so much room for growth and development that it is easy for the scope to quickly creep out of control. Due to this we outlined three specific deliverables that best encapsulate the overarching idea of the project.

  • Website
  • Database
  • API

Website
The website would be the first and most important deliverable, as it will serve as the main point of access for any brands or advertisers seeking to use the service. We will require a website that allows for the following functionality.

  • Account creation and secure login for brands and game developers.
  • Portal behind a secure login where customer specific information can be shown.
  • The ability to upload multimedia files (images and videos).
  • The ability to define the rules for how an advertisement is shown and to which demographic.


Database
We require a database that can receive images and details uploaded to the website. This is the second most important deliverable as we require somewhere to store all customer information and advertising data. The database will require the following functionality.

  • Receive and store information uploaded to the website.
  • Serve information upon request to the website.
  • Serve information upon request to a video game via API call.


API
Perhaps the most difficult part of the project will be the development of a custom API that will be used to pull advertising materials from the database and serve them into games. We will require the following functionality.

  • Get requests to retrieve data from the server.
  • Put requests to send data back to the server.

Once these 3 parts of the project are met, we will have developed a functional prototype that we can further expand upon. This will allow us to demonstrate a working product and let us provide potential employees or even investors with a proof of concept.
